The Red Shoes

Watched on February 17, 2026

In many ways this is exactly the kind of thing I’d consider one of the all time greatest movies. But the actual act of watching it was a little boring. Or not so much boring, but unsatisfying and mild. The beginning is slow, the melodrama seems to erupt out of nowhere at the end, the summary of “torn between her two loves” only seems to take up about 30 mins of this 132-minute film.

The dance in the middle is obviously stunning, and the restoration before/after for my 4k copy makes me so appreciative of the times I live in, where I can see this movie look better than maybe anyone has ever seen it since 1948.

It reminds me of Colonel Blimp in that, everything written about it afterwards convinces me fully that it’s a masterpiece, but my experience watching it was kind of muddled.

7/10
