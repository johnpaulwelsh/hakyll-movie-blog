All the hype and the accolades make me want to tear this apart. The comic relief sometimes falls flat (like Delta Slim almost gagging on the garlic). There's of course a distinct feeling that there's a thematic reason for the black, Irish, and vampire elements in this particular configuration, but the story doesn't seem to actually spend much time getting the audience to mull it over. Kayla says it's colonialism, like maybe? Also the entire first half would be a whole lot more boring if it weren't black people with cool voices and a lot of History behind what they're doing and saying.

But... Did I enjoy watching it? Hell yes I did, and that counts for a lot for me. Maybe I'll come around to it being great once I'm stop being told it's great, or once I read critical takes that I think are too harsh.

The fact that they needed to explain the vampire lore for the characters makes plot sense, but made me realize just how often movies assume that the characters know about them already, so it was weird to get a rundown of garlic, wood, silver, etc. when that part is usually skipped or played for winks. Also religion doesn't come into it, nobody says Christian iconography will work. That feels significant, that voodoo magic seems to work (Smoke can't bite Stack while he has the necklace on) but Jesus doesn't (they all recite the Our Father without feeling resistance). Although like, Christianity isn't actually a white people's religion historically so what is cooler trying to say about it's imposition on others and it's truth claims?

Coogler clearly wrote and shot that dance scene followed by the vamps showing up as some kind of mission statement and the most iconic scene of 2025. He might have succeeded.

But what is the significance of the snake that Stack stabs at the beginning???

8/10
