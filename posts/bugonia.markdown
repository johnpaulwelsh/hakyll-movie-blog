title: Bugonia
published: 2026-03-08

It's kind of shocking to me how similar this movie is to _Eddington_. They're both dark comedies, so dark that you might forget to laugh, but the overall tone is absurdity and a constant impish smirk. They both star Emma Stone, though in seemingly opposite roles. They are both about conspiracy theorists who think that some mysterious "other" force is putting them down and subjugating them to their nefarious whims. And in both movies the conspiracy ends up being true, but by then it's too late for our protagonist to do anything about it.

Between the two, I think _Eddington_ is more entertaining because _Bugonia_ just feels like it's going through the motions more, it has fewer surprises. Maybe part of this comes from watching it with subtitles, but I felt while watching that most of the scenes in the movie were only there to satisfy their purpose in the storyline, not to actually maintain my interest and bring the story in novel directions.

On the positive side, the music is absolutely hilarious (every time those big horns and strings come in I almost laughed), and of course the performances are really excellent.

I wonder if there's a way to read the film as, the CEO was never actually an alien and the guy really did accidentaly blow himself up in the closet. But right when his disembodied head hits her in the head, she loses all sanity and the rest of the film is her fantasy imagining of what could happen if she really were an alien.

One example of the movie being funny without having a lot of punchlines: the CEO tries to work her way out of the kidnapping situation by using corporate conflict-resolution speak, which is funny if she were a human but even funnier when she's really an alien.

6/10
