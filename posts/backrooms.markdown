I knew the bare minimum about the Backrooms pre-existing content before seeing the movie. All I really got the sense of was: analog horror, _Skinamarink_ vibes, and heavy lore. The risk with seeing a movie based on a pre-existing concept which doesn't already have a final status is, that the movie can feel like either one long ad for the rest of the content, which means that it's ultimately unsatisfying and incomplete, or else it can feel like a finale to that existing content, so you can sense that you're missing a lot of extra information that it's now not worth it for you to seek out since you already saw the ending. The fact that _Backrooms_ feels like neither of those kinds of movies is a major point in its favor.

I was nervous after the opening credits because why would I care about the personalities and interior states of the protagonists of a cosmic horror movie? The therapist scenes felt a little boring. But the genius 
f the movie is, the whole thing is about the interiority of its characters! There's a metaphor going on which they never explicitly spell out but is obvious enough for anyone who wants to think about it. I came to really appreciate the fact that you see the movie through Mary's eyes about as often as through Clark's, because the little glimpses of her backstory allow you to piece things together along with her.

The movie seems to be focused on the discomfort of being stuck inside a totally artificial space. Flourescent lights have  never been so menacing.

The first dive into the Backrooms is interesting but I could tell that they couldn't just do more silent awe-struck explorations for the rest of the movie, or else it would be boring. I think the movie successfully ramps up the horror and intrigue with each visit.

By the dinner table scene I was totally engaged. A solid mix of humor keeps the scene from being too earnest.

The final scenes where the scientists come into the fore, threaten to pull the movie off the rails, but thankfully almost nothing is explained. And the interiority of Mary's character is still front and center. She's still not able to escape buildings, after all this time.

The ending credits song, I think by Boards of Canada, is a banger. Best end credits song in a long time.

It would not surprise me if Kane Parsons had been directly influenced by all of these movies: _Inland Empire_, _Bugonia_, _Deep Red_, _Poltergeist_, _Twin Peaks: The Return_, _The Texas Chain Saw Massacre_, _Insidious_.

9/10
