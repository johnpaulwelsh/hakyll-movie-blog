Secondhand Lions

Watched on June 1, 2021

It’s a nice movie.

I absolutely fit this movie into the category of “kids stories where awkward kid is exposed to something quirky and challenging, to make him appreciate life better.” It’s a pretty good example of it too, but there are some aspects to it that really only make sense as a kids movie. Also I think it doesn’t quite lean far enough into the Big Fish themes of stories being more important than their truth, or maybe I’m just projecting that onto this movie because I like Big Fish so much.

Michael Caine does a great job of playing a southern American.

There are a lot of slice-of-life scenes that are really adorable, like the gardening scenes.

Haley Joel Osment is good in some scenes and kinda bad in others, it’s like as he got older he became more of a typical kid actor instead of the amazing kind of kid actor that he was in The Sixth Sense and AI: Artificial Intelligence.

The stuff with the mom and Walter were pretty implausible, I feel like he didn’t really do anything to become the kind of kid who could convince his mom to let him loose to live with his sketchy uncles.

7/10
