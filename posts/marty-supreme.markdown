I don't know, I feel like my critical thinking powers have dwindled as of late. I enjoyed the movie, I liked watching it. I was excited when I was supposed to be, I was contemplative when I was supposed to be, I laughed on cue, and I pondered the ending. But why do I feel like I need to have a more specific and personal take on this movie?

Maybe it's because I don't care about Marty's character arc, or if he ever actually has one. Will he be a good dad? I don't even think the movie is trying to get you to think that way. I think the point of Marty's character is, he's an unstoppable American jerk who can't help but come up short, and being anything other than the best is unacceptable to him. Maybe that's a metaphor? If so, why is it set in the 1950s? And why the 1980s pop songs mixed in? I mean it worked great, and wasn't even that distracting.

Point is, the movie is good but I can't figure out if I'll ever care about it again. _Uncut Gems_ didn't feel that way. Out of all the movies where "protag is a jerk and never learns his lesson" this sure is one of them.

There seems to be some thematic relevance to the fact that if Marty loses the Japanese show competition that he has to kiss a pig, given that he's Jewish and WWII is still fresh in people's minds.

8/10
